What it does

Designed for the pace
of an actual bar shift

No training required. No app to install. Works on any phone, from any browser, on the floor.

📱
Works on any device

A Progressive Web App — staff add it to their home screen from Safari or Chrome. No app store, no IT department, no install policy to navigate.

⚡
Orders in under 60 seconds

Items are pre-loaded per location. Staff tap to add, set quantity, and publish. The whole restock request takes less time than writing it on a slip of paper.

🔔
Instant push notifications

When an order is published, the whole team gets notified immediately — even with the app closed. No more checking paper logs or missed radio calls.

📊
Admin dashboard with analytics

See orders, transfers, and total value by day, week, or month. Top items by volume, and breakdowns by employee — all in the supervisor panel.

🧾
GL-coded reports

Every item carries a GL code and category. Export PDF or CSV with daily subtotals and GL summaries — ready to drop into your accounting workflow.

🔄
Transfers between locations

Log transfers between any two locations — Coffee Bar, Tea Bar, and satellite units. Every transfer is tracked, attributed, and included in cost reports.

How it works

Up and running
in one shift

No complex setup, no migration project. BarBackFlow is live the same day you decide to try it.

01
We configure your locations and item catalog

Your bar's products, GL codes, and unit pricing are loaded in before day one. Staff never see cost data — only the items they need to request.

02
Staff open the link, add their name, and start ordering

No account creation, no passwords. Staff bookmark the URL or add it to their home screen. First restock order goes out in minutes.

03
Supervisors log in and see everything

The admin panel shows live orders, historical reports, GL summaries, and employee-level breakdowns. Export to PDF or CSV on demand.

Why it matters

The operational return
is immediate

Less time spent on coordination. Fewer mistakes. A team that's aligned without a group chat thread for every restock.

Time spent on restock coordination

Every restock logged is a record of what moved and when. By the time inventory count comes around, you already have a detailed picture of consumption over the period — by item, by day, by GL category.

Each unit runs its own instance, with its own catalog and GL structure. Transfers between locations are tracked separately, so cross-unit movement is never lost in the noise.
